About

A Decade at the Intersection of Crypto and Policy

In 2015, Sung A (Sophie) Kim entered the cryptocurrency industry — years before most institutions even acknowledged its existence. She co-founded Hanbitco, becoming Korea's first female cryptocurrency exchange CEO, and built it into a fully regulated, VASP-licensed platform with ISO/IEC 27001 certification.

Recognizing that sustainable growth demanded clear rules, she turned to policy. She co-founded the Korea Blockchain Association and was elected by member exchanges as the inaugural Chair of its Exchange Committee — a role she held for three years. During this tenure, she played a central role in shaping Korea's landmark Special Financial Information Act, facilitating critical dialogue between industry leaders and government regulators.

Today, as founder of the International Digital Asset Policy Institute (IDAPI), she works at the nexus of global regulatory standards and Korea's domestic policy framework — conducting cross-border research, advising on institutional strategy, and ensuring Korea's digital asset ecosystem develops in alignment with international best practices.

"I believe the future of digital assets depends not just on innovation, but on the quality of the rules we build around it. My mission is to ensure those rules are informed, fair, and globally coherent."
